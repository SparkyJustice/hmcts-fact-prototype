Reading Magistrates' Court and Family Court

Types of cases heard: Children, Crime, Domestic Violence, Social Security

A Magistrates’ Court deals with less serious criminal offences such as minor theft, criminal damage, public disorder and motoring offences. There is no jury and often no judge but instead the case is heard by a panel of three trained up members of the local community that are called magistrates. Or a case can be heard by a district judge.

It also hears preliminary hearings for more serious criminal offences.

A Family Court helps families avoid disputes or if they should arise to resolve those problems quickly.
